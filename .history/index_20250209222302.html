Exploring Rental Affordability and cycling station near etablissement in France: An Interactive Data Visualization

Research Question: How affordable is it to rent in France?

In future implementation, we will explore the distribution of cycling stations near the university within each departement.
For the current version, we have implemented an interactive map of France, in which we can explore the rental affordability for each department.

Data: Departement data (departements.geojson) and Rental data (departement_rental_data.json)
which contains (Q1 and Q3 quantile, average rental price, Min & Max Rents and Department's Code).

Guidelines: When scrolling the mouse over the map, it will be pop a legend square including relevant information (box plot, minimum and maximum rent, etc) to answer our research question.

WHAT the visualization shows

This visualization focuses on an interactive map of France, highlighting the rental distribution across its departments (departements):

  • The map uses color gradients to reflect the median rental prices.
  • Hovering over a department displays a box plot with statistics such as minimum, maximum, and quartiles.
  • Additionally, it provides data on school distributions and cycling station locations, enabling users to explore convenience and transportation factors.

WHO the visualization is for

This visualization is designed for:

  • Students and academic staff: Quickly assess rental affordability across different regions and make location decisions based on school distribution and transportation convenience.
  • Renters, landlords, and real estate professionals: Gain an intuitive understanding of average rental prices across departments.
  • Policy makers: Observe rental disparities at a macro level to aid in urban planning and public infrastructure investments.

WHY the audience should care

  • Rental prices significantly affect people’s living and quality of life.
  • There are considerable variations in housing costs and income levels across France, and this map offers a clear visual representation of rental costs.
  • The distribution of schools and transportation hubs (e.g., cycling stations) further influences affordability and livability. Understanding these data points can help users make more informed and forward-thinking decisions regarding housing, work, or investment.

HOW to read the visualization

  1. Interactive map:
    • Hover your mouse over any department to see detailed box plot statistics (Min/Q1/Median/Q3/Max) and the average rental price. Darker colors generally indicate higher rental costs.
    • Clicking on a department zooms in and focuses on that area, showing its internal public/private schools and cycling station capacity.
  2. Legend and tooltips:
    • The legend on the right or bottom explains the meaning of the rental color scale, the distinction between high/low-capacity cycling stations, and the icons for public and private schools.
    • Hovering over icons reveals tooltips that display additional details about specific schools or stations.
  3. Pie Chart (if present):
    • A pie chart next to the map shows the distribution of schools across departments (or other related statistics). Departments with small proportions are grouped into “Others” to make the chart more comprehensible.
